Why Did So Many Companies Close?

Seven-plus companies disappearing from one metro area isn't random. There are a few reasons this happened:

  • National franchises couldn't make mid-sized markets work. Companies like DoodyCalls and POOP 911 relied on franchise operators who paid royalties and marketing fees. In a market like St. Louis, the math gets tight fast when you're splitting revenue with a corporate parent.
  • Solo operators burned out. Pet waste removal is a grind — it's outdoors, year-round, and weather doesn't care about your schedule. A lot of solo operators started strong and faded after a year or two.
  • No systems, no staying power. Many of these businesses didn't invest in scheduling software, route optimization, or customer management. That catches up with you.
  • COVID reshuffled the deck. Some operators pivoted to other work during the pandemic and never came back. Others lost enough customers during lockdowns that restarting didn't make financial sense.

The result? A metro area of nearly 3 million people with fewer than a handful of active pet waste removal providers. That's a problem if you're a dog owner, but it also means the companies that are still operating tend to be the ones that take it seriously.

What to Look for in a Pooper Scooper Service

With so many companies having disappeared, here's how to find one that'll actually stick around:

1. Check Their Online Presence

Is their website active? Is their Google Business listing claimed and updated with recent posts or reviews? A company that doesn't maintain their online presence probably isn't maintaining much else.

2. Look for Reviews — Recent Ones

A 5-star rating from 2022 means nothing if nobody's left a review since. Look for recent feedback. Active companies have fresh reviews because they're actively serving customers.

3. Ask About Contracts

Companies that require long-term contracts are often trying to lock you in because they know their service can't keep you otherwise. The best companies earn your business every visit. Look for month-to-month or no-contract options.

4. Local > National Franchise

This isn't always true everywhere, but the St. Louis pet waste removal market has proven it decisively. The national franchises left. The local operators stayed. When a local owner's name and reputation are on the line, they tend to work harder to keep you happy.

5. Communication Matters

Do they text you when service is complete? Can you easily reach someone by phone or text? One of the most common complaints about now-closed companies was "they just stopped showing up and I couldn't reach anyone."
