The Central County Yard Reality

Central County isn't West County. Yards here are smaller, more varied, more urban. A fenced backyard in Richmond Heights might be 800 square feet. A Ladue estate might be a full half-acre. And everything in between — townhome patios in Clayton, tightly fenced cottage lots in Maplewood, that perfect middle-of-the-neighborhood bungalow in University City.

We work in all of them. And we don't upcharge based on yard size. The pricing table above is the pricing. No "our tech will assess your yard and generate a custom quote." You have 1-2 dogs and you want weekly service? It's $70 a month. Done.

A few things unique to Central County that we've learned:

  • Gate access: A lot of Central County yards have alley gates or side-entry fences. Just let us know where to enter — we're used to creative access.
  • Shared spaces: If you have a shared yard or patio situation (condos, duplexes), let's talk. We can work around it or offer commercial pricing for common areas.
  • Dogs inside vs. outside: We ask that dogs be inside or in another area while we clean. It's safer and faster for everyone. Just tell us your setup when you sign up.
  • Leaf-heavy seasons: Fall in U-City and Maplewood means serious leaf coverage. We always check under the layers. This is a bigger deal than people realize with wood-chip landscaping or dense tree coverage.

Is a Pooper Scooper Service Worth It in Central County?

Depends on who you are. Honest answer.

It's probably worth it if:

  • You have a busy schedule and yard maintenance keeps falling to the bottom of the list
  • You've got more than one dog — accumulation gets out of hand fast
  • You have kids who use the backyard and stepping around is becoming a daily activity
  • You'd rather spend 5 minutes/week on something other than poop bags
  • You have someone who helps watch the house and a clean yard matters to you

It's probably not the right fit if:

  • You enjoy yard time with your dog and handle it as part of that routine
  • You have one very small dog and very little accumulation
  • You're in a strict budget situation and $70/month isn't in the cards right now

We're not going to pressure anyone. But most customers who sign up don't cancel — not because we trap them (there's no contract), but because having it done every week turns out to be way more valuable than they expected when they signed up.
