The February Yard Situation in St. Louis

Here's the thing about Valentine's Day specifically: February 14 in St. Louis is the tail end of winter. Your dog has been using the backyard every day since the weather turned cold in October or November — and in cold temperatures, waste doesn't decompose. It just accumulates.

❄️ Why February is the Worst Month for Dog Owners

Cold temperatures halt decomposition almost entirely. A deposit left in November is still there in February, preserved by the cold. By Valentine's Day, most St. Louis dog owners have 3–4 months of winter accumulation in their yard — sometimes 100+ deposits per dog just from the winter alone. The yard that looked manageable in September is a different situation entirely by February.

A first cleanup gift in February doesn't just make the yard look nice for a week. It clears months of winter buildup in one visit, then keeps it clean every week after that heading into spring. By the time March comes and everyone wants to be outside again, the yard is already ready.
