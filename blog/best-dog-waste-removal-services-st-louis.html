What to Actually Look For in a Dog Waste Service

Most people pick based on price, which makes sense. But there are four factors that determine whether you'll actually be happy with the service six months in:

💲
Flat Rate vs. Per-Visit
Per-visit pricing can sound cheaper but adds up. 4 visits/month at $19/visit = $76. Flat monthly rates are more predictable and usually cheaper annually. Look for a service that charges the same amount regardless of how many times they come.
📱
Communication System
The single biggest complaint about pet waste services: "I don't know if they came." A service that texts before arriving and after finishing is worth a few extra dollars per month. You should never have to check your yard to verify they were there.
📍
Confirmed Coverage
National franchises list huge territories but often have spotty coverage in specific neighborhoods. Always confirm your exact address is on the route — not just that they "serve St. Louis." Ask specifically about your zip code and neighborhood.
🤝
Local vs. Franchise
As we saw with POOP 911 and DoodyCalls: national franchises can exit a market without warning. A local owner-operator has more accountability — it's their business, their reputation, and their community. You're also more likely to get a direct text back instead of a ticket system.
📝
Contract Terms
Avoid services that require long-term contracts. Good services don't need to trap customers — they earn repeat business through reliability. A month-to-month service with no cancellation fee is a sign of confidence in their own work.
🚛
Waste Disposal
Ask what happens to the waste. Some services bag it and leave the bags at the edge of your yard (not ideal). The better approach: waste leaves your property entirely with the service provider. Ask explicitly how they handle disposal.
