Why South City Is Different (and Why That Matters for Your Yard)

Every suburban service can handle a ranch house with a 50×50 fenced backyard. South City is a different animal.

Small Yards, Dense Dogs

South City lots average less than 1,500 square feet — but the dogs using them are full-size. A German Shepherd in a Tower Grove rowhouse produces the same 300+ deposits per year as one in a Chesterfield subdivision, just concentrated in a much smaller footprint. The smell-to-square-foot ratio is not in your favor.

📐 South City Yard Reality

The average South City fenced yard is roughly 20×40 feet. At 1 dog = 300 annual deposits, that's one pile for roughly every 2.6 square feet of usable yard. You can't host a cookout in a minefield. Weekly service means your yard is actually usable.

Alley Access & Gate Logistics

Most suburban services have never dealt with an alley-gated yard. They show up at the front door, can't find the yard, and give up. South City yards are accessed through alleys, side gates, or shared driveways. We note it on your account and handle it exactly the same way every week — no fumbling, no missed visits.

The Renter Situation

A lot of South City dogs live in rented properties. The yard technically belongs to the landlord, the cleanup responsibility is a gray area, and the result is a six-month backlog. You don't need your landlord's permission to hire us — if you have yard access, we can service it. Dozens of South City renters pay us directly.

Shared Yards and Multi-Unit Buildings

Some South City properties have shared courtyards, shared backyards, or multi-unit access points. We handle those too — coordinate with your neighbors, split the cost, and call us once. We'll figure out the logistics.
