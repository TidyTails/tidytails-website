Why North County Has Had a Dog Waste Problem

North County is dog country. Drive through any neighborhood — Florissant, Hazelwood, Ferguson, Jennings — and you'll see dogs in every yard, at every dog park, on every block. But historically, the pet service industry has underserved this part of St. Louis County.

The bigger national pooper scooper franchises concentrate their routes where density and income levels make the route math easy. That means south county and west county tend to get the coverage, while places like Florissant (one of the largest cities in Missouri, by the way) get deprioritized.

The result? A lot of dog owners up here either do it themselves — which works until it doesn't — or they just live with the problem. Especially in winter, when nobody wants to go outside, and then spring hits and the yard looks like a mine field.

If that sounds like your backyard right now, we get it. That's literally why we're expanding north.

Is a Pooper Scooper Service Worth It for North County Dog Owners?

The honest answer depends on your situation. Here's the version we'd give a friend:

It's absolutely worth it if:

  • You have 2+ dogs and a medium-to-large yard
  • You have kids who play outside
  • You travel or work long hours and the yard gets away from you
  • You've ever stepped in something and sworn for 10 minutes
  • Your HOA has sent you a letter (yes, that happens)

It might not be for you if:
