The Three Reasons Pet Waste Becomes a Commercial Problem

Before we get into how the service works, it's worth naming why this problem tends to escalate on managed properties specifically — because the fix needs to address all three.

📣

Tenant Complaints

Pet waste is the #1 pet-related maintenance complaint at multifamily properties nationally. Residents track it on shoes, kids contact it on playgrounds, and neighbors file complaints about odor during warm months. The complaints don't stop until the waste does.

⚖️

Liability Exposure

Dog waste contains E. coli, salmonella, roundworm, and other pathogens. A slip-and-fall incident on a contaminated walkway, or a child's infection traced to a common lawn area, creates documented liability for the property. Regular professional cleanup is the paper trail that shows you maintained the grounds.

📋

Lease Enforcement

Pet addenda are only as effective as your ability to enforce them. When common areas are professionally cleaned on a documented schedule, your management team has a clear baseline to point to when issuing lease violation notices to problem pet owners. "The area was cleaned Tuesday. This photo is from Thursday." That's a conversation-ending paper trail.

⚠️ The Health Risk Is Real

The EPA classifies pet waste as a non-point source water quality pollutant under the Clean Water Act — in the same category as oil runoff and pesticides. Properties with documented pet waste accumulation on grounds that drain to municipal storm systems can face regulatory exposure in addition to the tenant complaint and liability picture. Regular removal eliminates that risk at the source.

Why Local Ownership Matters for Commercial Accounts

This comes up in every commercial conversation, and it's worth addressing directly. National pet waste removal franchises exist, they're legitimate businesses, and in some markets they're the only option. In St. Louis, you have a local alternative.

Here's what local ownership changes for a commercial account:

  • Same-day problem resolution. When there's an issue — a service day conflict, a coverage area question, a complaint from a tenant about a spot that was missed — you text one number and get an answer the same day. No regional dispatch, no "we'll have someone call you."
  • No franchise overhead in your rate. National franchise pricing reflects the royalty structure the franchisee is paying to the parent company. Local service eliminates that layer. You pay for the service, not the brand.
  • Accountability that doesn't rely on corporate policy. Our reputation in St. Louis is the only thing we have. A national brand can absorb a bad account — we can't afford one. That difference in stakes is real and it affects how we show up.
  • Flexible arrangements. No standard franchise contract template. If you need 2x/week during summer and 1x/week during winter, we can build that. If you have three properties and need a custom invoicing arrangement, we can do that. These conversations happen with the owner, not with a contract template.
