South County: High Dog Density, Under-Served by Waste Removal

Here's something worth knowing: South St. Louis County has one of the highest concentrations of single-family homes with fenced yards in the entire metro area. That's a good thing — it means dogs have space to run. It also means waste accumulates fast, especially in yards under a quarter acre where a single medium-sized dog can leave over 700 deposits per year.

The national pet waste removal franchises have always been less focused on South County compared to, say, the I-64 corridor or Clayton. The reason is route math — it's harder to cluster a large number of stops in a geographically spread-out area. As a result, the local operators who used to fill that gap have either grown, gotten overwhelmed, or stopped operating entirely.

What that leaves is a real service gap. Dog owners in Kirkwood, Crestwood, Webster Groves, and Mehlville are either doing it themselves, paying way too much for inconsistent service, or going without.
